How Much Is the Rebate?

  • $3,000 — Standard rebate for any qualifying Maine homeowner

What Qualifies?

  • Maine primary residence (not seasonal/rental)
  • Installation by an Efficiency Maine-approved HVAC contractor

  • Rebate application submitted within 90 days of install

Storm Roof Damage? We Match You With Insurance-Savvy Maine Roofers

Wind, hail, and ice dams are a reality for Maine homes. If your roof took a hit, your homeowners insurance almost certainly covers it — you pay only your deductible, the insurer covers the rest. The catch: you need a roofer who understands the claim process and will advocate for the full repair scope.

What Storm Damage Is Covered?

  • Wind damage (missing or lifted shingles, flashing failures)
  • Hail impact damage (granule loss, bruising, punctures)
  • Ice dam damage (water infiltration, fascia/soffit rot)
  • Falling tree/branch impact

The Insurance Claim Process — Simplified

  1. Document damage with photos immediately after the storm
  2. File a claim with your homeowners insurer (call or use their app)
  3. Schedule an adjuster inspection — have a roofer present
  4. Your matched contractor provides a scope-of-work estimate
  5. Insurer approves; roofer completes repair; you pay only deductible

Our matched roofers have handled Maine insurance claims before. They know which damage adjusters overlook, how to document properly, and how to prevent claim underpayment. They meet the adjuster on your behalf.
